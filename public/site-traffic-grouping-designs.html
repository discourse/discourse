UI/UX exploration · Site traffic · Admin dashboard

Bucket grouping: day / week / month

The site-traffic chart aggregates pageviews into bars. Adding weekly bucketing for in-between ranges raises two questions: (1) do admins pick the bucket, or does the system? (2) how does each individual bar communicate what it represents — since a "weekly bar" reading 24,500 is the sum of seven days, not one. Options A–D below cover the first question; the tooltip section covers the second. Try the period selector, the bucket controls, the filter pills, and hover any bar to see the tooltip.

Constraint to keep in mind. Bar density is the real readability driver: ≈ 7–90 bars reads cleanly, ≈ 180 daily bars (Last 90 days) is the upper bound, ≈ 365 daily bars is unreadable. Try Last 12 months → Day in Option B to feel the failure mode.

Option A — Adaptive only

Lowest friction Try the period selector

The system chooses the bucket from the period length and a small caption underneath the chart says what it picked. Daily for ≤ 31 days, weekly for 32–183 days, monthly for > 183 days.

Pros

  • Zero learning curve. Admins never have to think about bucket size.
  • Impossible to land on a broken view (300+ daily bars on a long range).
  • Smallest UI surface — period selector stays the only chart control.
  • Matches Stripe, Plausible, GA4 default behaviour.

Cons

  • Power admins lose the ability to see daily detail inside a long custom range.
  • The current spec (§7.2) explicitly rejects weekly bucketing — adding it back here means a content change, not just UI.
  • Caption text is easy to miss; some admins may misread weekly bars as daily.
Best when: v1, where simplicity beats flexibility.

Option B — Explicit segmented control

Maximum control Try Day · Week · Month

A Day · Week · Month segmented control sits next to the period selector. Options that don't make sense for the current period are disabled, not hidden, so the control's shape stays predictable.

Pros

  • Direct, recognisable. Admins see exactly what they'll get and can switch instantly.
  • Useful escape hatch: a 6-month custom investigation can still see daily detail.
  • Disabled-not-hidden states keep the toolbar shape stable as periods change.

Cons

  • Adds a second chart-shaping control — twice the cognitive load on first load.
  • Daily on Last 12 months is technically possible (~365 bars). Either we cap it or ship a knowingly-busy chart.
  • Persisting a sticky choice across sessions risks “why is my chart suddenly all bars?”
  • Mobile cost: two control groups have to wrap on narrow screens.
Best when: the audience is power-admin heavy and frequently inspects custom ranges. Probably more control than v1 needs.

Option C — Smart default with low-emphasis override

Recommended Click the caption ↓

The system picks the bucket; the bucket name below the chart is a clickable button that opens a Day / Week / Month popover. The default looks indistinguishable from Option A.

Pros

  • Default experience is identical to Option A — no extra control to read.
  • Override is discoverable without competing for the period selector's attention.
  • Override stays a single click away when an admin needs to verify a different aggregation.

Cons

  • One more interaction layer to design and test (popover, focus management, keyboard).
  • Caption-as-control is less common; some admins won't realise they can change it.
  • Hover-only would fail accessibility — must be focusable and clickable.
Best when: we want the default to feel like a one-control section, but still let curious admins drill in.

Option D — Period selector encodes both

Most opinionated Open the dropdown

Bucket choice is folded into the period selector itself. Each preset implies its own bucket and the menu labels say so out loud (“Last 90 days · weekly”).

Pros

  • Single chart-shaping control. Period and bucket move as one decision.
  • Menu labels educate admins about how the chart aggregates without extra captions.
  • Especially neat on mobile — only one dropdown to fit.

Cons

  • No way to override at all from the dashboard.
  • Locks bucket to period — admins surveying a 4-month custom investigation can't drop to daily.
  • Slightly noisy preset labels for the 90% who never think about it.
Best when: the dashboard is read-only and we want to actively resist power-admin tweaking.

Tooltip design — communicating the bucket per-bar

Cross-cutting Hover any bar above

Whichever option ships, the chart-level caption only tells admins what aggregation is active globally — each individual bar still has to communicate what it represents. A weekly bar showing “24,500” should read at a glance as a 7-day sum, not a single day's count. The tooltip carries that signal three ways:

1. Title carries the bucket

Daily: Mon, 5 May 2026 (day of week + date). Weekly: a date range — 27 Apr – 3 May 2026. Monthly: May 2026. The title shape alone identifies the bucket size — no separate “Daily/Weekly/Monthly” sub-header needed.

2. Partial coverage, in a red pill

When a bucket is cut short by today or by the period boundary, a red pill below the title names the actual coverage: Mon – Wed for a partial week, 1 – 6 May for a partial month, today for the partial daily bar. Pairs with the bar's reduced opacity.

3. Humans vs crawlers

“Pageviews” (logged in + anonymous) sums to a bold total. When the Crawlers filter is active, crawlers render below a dashed divider with their own bold total, making it visible that the headline number doesn't include bots.

Why this matters: the tooltip is the only place where a bar's bucket size can be stated unambiguously alongside its number. Without the sub-header line, admins lose the connection between the chart-level grouping decision (Option A–D) and the per-bar reading.

Recommendation

Ship Option C — Smart default with low-emphasis override for v1, paired with the tooltip design above. Specifics:

Bucket selection (chart-level)

  • Default rule: daily ≤ 31 days · weekly 32–183 days · monthly > 183 days. Same rule for custom ranges of equivalent length.
  • Caption-as-control: Showing weekly totals sits below the chart. The bucket name itself is the button — its underline is the only affordance — opening a 3-item popover (Day / Week / Month). The current selection is highlighted; nonsense options are disabled with a brief reason.
  • Disable, don't hide, options that would render nonsense (“Month” on Last 7 days). Show the disabled reason in the row's helper text.
  • Don't persist a manual override across sessions; reset on period change.
  • Accessibility: caption is a <button>, popover is a roving-tab-index listbox, disabled rows have aria-disabled.

Tooltip (per-bar communication)

  • Title format follows the bucket: Tue, 5 May 2026 (daily, day of week + date) · 27 Apr – 3 May 2026 (weekly, as a date range) · May 2026 (monthly). The title shape alone identifies the bucket — no “Daily/Weekly/Monthly” sub-header in normal cases.
  • Partial coverage shown in a red pill below the title — names the actual coverage rather than describing the gap. Weekly: Mon – Wed or Wed – Sun. Monthly: 1 – 6 May. Daily: today. Pairs with reduced bar opacity. The cause (in-progress vs. range-cut) is implicit from the bar's position in the chart, so the sub-line stays minimal.
  • Pageviews / Crawlers separation: humans (logged in + anonymous) sum to a bold “Pageviews” total; crawlers, when active, render below a dashed divider so admins always see that the headline number doesn't include bots.
  • Hidden series stay hidden: a deactivated filter pill removes its row from the tooltip too — no “0 logged in” line when the series is off.
  • Touch + keyboard: tap-to-pin on touch devices; bars are focusable so keyboard users get the same tooltip.
